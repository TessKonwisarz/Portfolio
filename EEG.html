EEG Data Visualization¶

EEG data visualization is useful to find peaks and troughs in the ERP channel data to determine differences in activity patterns. Activity patterns will tell you which parts of the brain are most activated and when to allow interpretation of any underlying processes in the brain.

A subset of the 'EEG data from basic sensory task in Schizophrenia' dataset was uploaded from Kaggle.com. Subject neural activity was studied to determine a difference in ERPs between control subjects and patients with Schizophrenia in a button press and auditory task. Only the data from the first two subjects were uploaded along with the neural activity for two out of three conditions.

The study found that when comparing condition 1 to condition 2, the control group experienced a negative deflection at N100, found approximately 100ms after stimulus onset, however the patient group did not experience such a deflection. Although it is not necessariily clear whether the results are significant when viewing the group data from only two participants for each group, there is a visible difference in the N100 deflection between groups, where it appears that the patient group experiences a less pronounce delfection at N100 compared to the control group.
